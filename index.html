Back to Acadien Roots

Shediac's orignins go back to the 1800's century when its main purpose was to take advantage of it marinas to supply French troops overseas. It is not until the mid 1800's when Acadiens began settling down and building a local working force revolving around fishing and labour related work. With the arrival of the maritime's first railway system in Canada, Shediac soon became an integral part of New-Brunswick's legacy. One of the most treasured remains of Shediac's begining is its old train station that serves as a museum today.

Bake, Steam or Boil

It is only fitting that Shediac have a nickname such as "The lobster capital of the world". The abundance of fishing allows for fisherman, lobster plant owners, and seafood lovers to call the Shediac bay their second home. In fact, a visitor can expect to be greeted by a 90 tonnes lobster statue that declares itself as the world's biggest lobster sitting on the shore of the bay. To celebrate Shediac's pride of seafood and fishing, an annual Lobster Festival brings the locals and tourists together for breaking records of the longest lobster roll, lobster eating contests and shucking competitions.
